Linen

Linen, a natural fiber derived from the flax plant (a bast fiber), is highly valued under the CPHFW sustainability framework for its low environmental impact compared to conventional cotton. Since flax requires minimal water, few pesticides, and can often be grown in rotation, linen contributes positively to soil health and regenerative agriculture practices.

However, CPHFW encourages brands to seek certified linen to ensure sustainable processing and compliance with social standards throughout the supply chain.

Prioritizing linen demonstrates a brand's commitment to using durable, natural fibers and reducing reliance on resource-intensive materials, aligning perfectly with the event's goals for sustainable fashion transformation.
