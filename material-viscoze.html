Viscoze

Viscose, a type of regenerated cellulose fiber, is a key focus area under the CPHFW sustainability requirements due to the environmental risk posed by its production. Conventional viscose manufacturing often involves the use of toxic chemicals and contributes to deforestation by sourcing pulp from endangered forests.

To comply with CPHFW’s minimum standards for certified materials (at least 60% by 2025), brands must prioritize sustainable alternatives like LENZING™ ECOVERO™ or TENCEL™ Lyocell.

These preferred fibers mitigate impact by using FSC/PEFC-certified wood pulp and closed-loop production systems, showcasing a commitment to responsible sourcing and chemical reduction in the fashion supply chain.
